Why I love computer science

by Samira C

My introduction to tech

My introduction to the world of technology was with Girls Who Code in high school when I was a student in the GWC Summer Immersion Program. Prior to this summer, I never envisioned myself in tech. However, learning alongside a classroom of other women, many of whom were also beginning their computer science journeys, I felt empowered and discovered very quickly that tech was for me. Through this program, I met one of my cherished mentors, and she is a big reason for why I am still in tech. Being in an inclusive and welcoming environment is crucial, especially when tackling new fields such as like AI and machine learning. Being a part of this supportive environment will make the biggest difference.

What is exciting to me in tech

In many of my previous internships and projects, I have mainly focused on web development, which I have also loved. However, I want to make a transition to the data science/AI/ML path. To me, computer science has endless possibilities, and these fields in particular get me excited to learn and explore!

Though AI sounds exciting and full of so many possibilities, it is a little scary. The reason for this is that it seems machine learning researchers themselves don't completely understand the inner workings of how and why a model works the way it does. I think this is quite alarming and tells us that us as technologists need to step back and evaluate the true power of what we are creating. AI is great and all, but to what extent should we limit it so that it doesn't lose our control and oversight? It's alarming to hear about many companies firing their AI ethics and implicit bias researchers. As these technologies are continuing to become integrated into our everyday lives (they already have been for some time), we need to analyze whether these technologies work for everybody or just the select few. As someone entering this space, I want to ensure that we as a society don't fail to recognize the importance of creating AI that is ethical and works for all. I am truly excited to learn and delve deeper into AI and machine learning, and ultimately contribute to the future of technology.
